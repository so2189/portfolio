    • Motivational Bookshelf

    A collaborative creative physical computing and fabrication project that encpurages reading. uses weight sensors and servo motors to reveal hidden flower elements based on book placement.

    Team Members

    Christina Woo, Sanjida Orpi, Jenesis Blancaflor, Isabella Menshouse, KJ Moses, Sabria Islam

    Overview

    Motivational Bookshelf integrates sensors, servo motors, and a custom-designed wooden structure to transform the act of placing books into an interactive, uplifting experience. When a book is placed, flowers rise from the shelf to visually reward the action.

    Development Timeline

    • Week 1–2: Planning and initial sensor/gear exploration, rack-and-pinion design
    • Week 3–4: Prototyping gear and rail, calibrating weight sensors with HX711 modules
    • Week 5–6: Testing servo motor integration with sensor input, refining laser-cut pieces
    • Week 7–8: Final assembly, redesign for support and stability, acrylic flower construction
    • Spring 2025: Completed the full acrylic box structure and began servo testing via button inputs

    Features

    • ✦ Weight-sensor based flower activation
    • ✦ Custom laser-cut gear and rail mechanism
    • ✦ Servo-controlled flower movements (up/down)
    • ✦ Adjustable acrylic stabilizers for flower stems
    • ✦ Modular design for future scaling

    In Progress

    We continue to iterate on flower support structure, fine-tune the motor-to-sensor code, and finalize user-trigger mechanisms. The final version will allow books placed on specific sections of the shelf to trigger servo-driven blooms.
