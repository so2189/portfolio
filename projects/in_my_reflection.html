    • In My Reflection

    An interactive art installation created to promote self-reflection through immersive audiovisual interaction. The project transforms a live camera feed into a pixelated, water-like reflection of the user and integrates voice-based responses into the experience using p5.js and ml5.js libraries. This audio-based reflective experience journals your vocal responses and was created for a course called Interactive Computing with one partner.

    Visit Site

    Overview

    The In My Reflection Experience is an interactive artwork that uses water as a metaphor for introspection. Viewers are invited to verbally respond to a series of reflective questions while seeing their likeness mirrored in a dynamic, pixelated water surface. Their spoken words appear as rippling text within the reflection, symbolizing how thoughts and emotions shape one’s self-image.

    Motivation

    Inspired by the reflective qualities of water, our goal was to create an experience that visually represents personal reflection. We wanted users to see themselves—literally and figuratively—while responding to questions that promote mindfulness and self-awareness. The project combines computer vision, speech recognition, and generative art to create an experience that evolves with each participant.

    Methods & Tools

    • ✦ Pixelated video reflection using createCapture() and pointillism
    • ✦ Dynamic text effects via textToPoints() and sin() oscillation
    • ✦ ml5.js PoseNet and Body Segmentation for real-time body tracking
    • ✦ p5.speech library for audio recognition and speech-to-text
    • ✦ Ripple effects and wave animation using Perlin noise and sine functions

    Process & Updates

    Early stages involved setting up the camera input and testing pixelation effects. We explored methods to visualize users’ reflections using pointillism and experimented with wave animations inspired by oscillating dot patterns. We also began integrating ml5 PoseNet for facial tracking and considered how to segment the user’s head and shoulders from the background.

    The final experience features four reflective questions navigated via arrow buttons. Users activate the microphone to respond verbally, and their speech is transcribed and visualized as wave-like text around their reflection. Body segmentation masks non-body pixels, simulating a watery mirror. Text is positioned dynamically around the user’s face using PoseNet ear tracking. Additional enhancements include a ripple animation object that reacts to mouse presses and a start/end screen to introduce and conclude the experience.

    Performance optimizations involved using alpha-mapped masks instead of multiple buffer layers to reduce lag. Future improvements include more precise facial mapping, transparent background segmentation, and additional question sets themed around personal, collective, and environmental reflection.

    Individual Contributions

    I developed the button and microphone interface for smooth question navigation, implemented oscillating text effects using sin(), and integrated ml5 Body Pose for ear-based tracking of user reflections. I optimized body segmentation by refining alpha masks and designed the HTML/CSS interface for the project. Additionally, I handled audio playback management and incorporated sound effects during save interactions.
